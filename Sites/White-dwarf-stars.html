White Dwarfs

A white dwarf is a star that was normal in size but collapsed into a very small dwarf star after it ran out of nuclear fuel. A typical white dwarf has a radius that is 1 percent that of the Sun, but it has roughly the same mass. This corresponds to a density of about 1 ton per cubic centimeter.

The material in a white dwarf no longer undergoes fusion reactions, so the star has no source of energy. As a result, it cannot support itself by the heat generated by fusion against gravitational collapse, but is supported only by electron degeneracy pressure, causing it to be extremely dense.
